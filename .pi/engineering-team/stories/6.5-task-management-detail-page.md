# Story 6.5: Role-based task management on the detail page

**Status:** Done
**Created:** 2026-06-03
**Type:** Feature

## Background
Story 2.5 put the Patron's "assign/change arbiter" control on the task detail page (`/task/:naddr`). But that's one action of many: a Catallax task moves `proposed → funded → in_progress → submitted → concluded`, and each transition is taken by a specific role (Patron, Arbiter, or Worker). Today there's no in-app way to drive the rest of that loop — you'd have to hand-author events. This story makes the detail page the single place where each logged-in user can see and perform exactly the actions their role permits on *this* task, so the whole lifecycle is drivable end-to-end through the UI.

Protocol grounding (from `NIP.md` + `src/lib/catallax.ts`): task status lives on the replaceable kind-33401 and is updated by re-publishing it; relays/consumers accept an update only from an **authorized updater** — the task's patron, arbiter, or worker (so the *acting role signs its own transition*). The terminal step is a kind-3402 **conclusion** carrying a `resolution` of `successful | rejected | cancelled | abandoned`, a payout/refund zap-receipt reference, and the task moving to `concluded`. "The patron or arbiter marks the task as `funded`." This is base-Catallax, shared with upstream — no Grantless-specific privilege.

This story does **not** build crowdfunding (Story 7) or real Lightning. To keep the loop drivable now, a manual "mark funded" action is included (the *act* of flipping status); the contribution/goal mechanics and real payments remain Story 7 / a later hardening pass, with payout/refund **mocked** here as the seed and nak harness already model them.

## User-facing description
As a logged-in participant in a task — its Patron, Arbiter, or Worker — I want the task's detail page to show me exactly the actions I'm allowed to take at its current stage and let me take them, so that a project can move all the way from proposed to concluded through the UI, with every role's part visible to everyone.

## Acceptance criteria
- [ ] **Actions are gated by role *and* current status.** Given a logged-in user on a task's detail page, then the page offers only the actions valid for that user's role on this task at its current status, and offers **no** management actions to a user who holds no role on the task (or who is logged out). Each action below appears only in the status where it applies.
- [ ] **Mark funded (Patron or Arbiter, `proposed` → `funded`).** Given the task's patron *or* arbiter on a `proposed` task, when they mark it funded, then the kind-33401 is re-published **signed by that actor** with status `funded` (all other fields preserved); only the latest version is authoritative and every viewer sees `funded`.
- [ ] **Assign a worker (Patron, `funded` → `in_progress`).** Given the patron on a `funded` task, when they assign a worker — a pubkey that **may be the patron themselves** (self-assignment) — then the 33401 is re-published (patron-signed) with the worker as the third `p` tag and status `in_progress`; the worker is surfaced to all viewers.
- [ ] **Mark submitted (Worker, `in_progress` → `submitted`).** Given the task's worker on an `in_progress` task, when they mark the work submitted, then the 33401 is re-published **worker-signed** with status `submitted`.
- [ ] **Conclude (Arbiter, `submitted` → `concluded`).** Given the task's arbiter on a `submitted` task, when they conclude with a chosen resolution (`successful`, `rejected`, `cancelled`, or `abandoned`), then a kind-3402 is published **arbiter-signed** that references the task (its `a`-coordinate + event id) and a **mocked** payout/refund zap receipt, and the task moves to `concluded`; the resolution is shown on the page.
- [ ] **The full loop is drivable end-to-end.** Given the seeded actors, the task can be taken `proposed → funded → in_progress → submitted → concluded` entirely from the detail page, each transition performed by the correct role, with no stale status leaking (latest version wins at every step).
- [ ] **Roles and relationships are surfaced.** Given a task, then its Patron, Arbiter, and Worker are shown plainly (name/avatar with fallback); where the same pubkey holds more than one role (e.g. proposer is also the worker, or also the arbiter), that is shown rather than hidden — the Web of Trust judges it.
- [ ] **No privileged actor (openness).** Given the above, then permission to act comes solely from the protocol's authorized-updater rule (holding a role on the task), never from any client-side allowlist or special-cased pubkey/relay; the acting user signs their own event (explicit signing, no nsec exposure); writes go to the app's configured, overridable relay. Anyone could run their own instance and drive the same loop.

## Out of scope
- **Crowdfunding contributions, goal-met detection, and real Lightning** payout/refund — Story 7 and a later hardening pass. Here, "mark funded" is a manual status action and payout/refund are mocked zap receipts (as in the seed/harness).
- **Editing a task's content fields** (title/description/requirements/amount/deadline) after creation — a separate CRUD-Update story (per planning decision).
- **Assign/change arbiter** — already delivered (Story 6 + surfaced on the detail page in Story 2.5); this story surfaces it alongside the others but does not rebuild it.
- **A worker "applying"/accepting** flow — like arbiter assent, worker agreement is out-of-band; assignment is the patron's action (which may be self-assignment).
- **Disputes/appeals** beyond the four `resolution` values; **un-concluding** or reopening a concluded task.
- **Proportional refund math** — the funding/refund amounts are Story 7's concern; here the conclusion records the resolution + a mocked receipt.

## Openness check
Every action is permitted purely by the Catallax authorized-updater rule (the acting user holds that role on the task) — there is no client-side gatekeeper, allowlist, or special pubkey. Risky relationships (proposer = worker, arbiter = proposer) are **surfaced, not blocked** — consistent with the prime directive and the "no `arbiter ≠ team` enforcement" decision. The acting user signs their own 33401/3402 via their signer (no nsec exposure, explicit confirmation). Reads/writes use the configured, overridable relay. A forker running their own instance, relays, and actors drives the identical loop. Consistent with the prime directive.

## Open questions
Resolved during planning:
- **CRUD-Update scope:** lifecycle + role assignments only; editing task content fields is deferred to its own story.
- **`funded` transition:** include a manual "mark funded" (patron or arbiter) so the loop is testable now; real funding is Story 7.
- **Conclusion payout/refund:** mocked zap receipt referenced by the 3402 (matching the seed/harness); real Lightning later.
- **Who may assign the worker:** the patron (self-assignment allowed); a separate worker-applies flow is out of scope.
- **"Delete"/cancel:** expressed as an arbiter conclusion with resolution `cancelled`/`abandoned` (the protocol's path); no separate unilateral delete.
